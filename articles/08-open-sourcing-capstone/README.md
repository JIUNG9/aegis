The capstone of the Aegis v4.0 article series. Unlike Articles 1-7, which are technical deep-dives on individual layers, this piece is the career-narrative wrapper. ## LinkedIn cross-post strategy

This article is the one Article-8-flagged piece to post as a **full LinkedIn Article** (not just a post). The other seven in the series go out as LinkedIn posts with a pull quote plus link to Medium. This one gets the Article treatment because:

1. The audience is recruiters, and recruiters read LinkedIn natively
2. Long-form on LinkedIn gets indexed by Recruiter and surfaces on profile searches
3. The CTA at the bottom ("for Canadian recruiters reading this") is the whole point

LinkedIn Article headline: *"Open-Sourcing an AI-Native DevSecOps Platform — From Side Project to Canada Relocation Portfolio"* — slightly different from the Medium title so the same reader sees fresh framing in both places.

Pair the LinkedIn Article publication with a companion feed post: three-paragraph version + headline image (mermaid architecture diagram exported to PNG) + link.

## Timing

**Publish after Articles 1-7 have all gone live.** This is the capstone; it references every previous article. Releasing it before the series is complete reduces its weight and forces the reader to imagine the supporting work instead of clicking through to it.

Suggested order: ship Articles 1-7 over four weeks at a cadence of two per week, then publish Article 8 the following Monday as the finale. LinkedIn post lands the same day.

3,200 to 3,800 words. This is the longest article in the series by design — it has to tell a career story, not just explain a pattern.
